 Welcome!

Course Description

This intensive workshop introduces students to composing and performing electronic music in real time using Ableton Live’s Session View. Students will learn to build, manipulate, and arrange loops and clips on the fly and integrate Ableton Live's clip view to create music. We’ll begin by establishing foundational skills working with audio and MIDI clips before moving into ambient, generative, and hybrid performance and composition strategies.

This course is a workshop that will start with demonstrations of key concepts and some in-class creation. Students will get personalized feedback on their work and guidance for new techniques applicable to their interests, and will be expected to work on their music outside of class.

Familiarity with electronic music or music in general is welcome. No prior experience with Ableton is required, but this course will move quickly and should be helpful to both new users and experienced Ableton users. We will discuss performance techniques but will also focus on compositional strategies.

Computers and wired headphones will be required for the class. There may be some headphones available to borrow for in-class use, and the school has options for borrowing computers.


Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Navigate Ableton Live Session View confidently for live performance and composition.
  • Create a solo or collaborative live performance set.
  • Use generative techniques to create music and audio.
  • Integrate personal artistic voices into live electronic music contexts and develop a vocabulary for listening to, analyzing, and creating electronic music
  • Build, record, trigger, and manipulate audio and MIDI clips in real time.

What to bring to class

Please bring a computer and headphones to class!

If you don't have a computer, you can check out one for 21 days from the school library! https://library.calarts.edu/services/borrowlaptop

We may or may not have a couple pairs of headphones from MTEC that we can lend for the first class, so please bring your own.

Reminder that Bluetooth headphones (including Apple Airpods!) don't really work for real-time music making, since audio over Bluetooth has high and variable latency times.
